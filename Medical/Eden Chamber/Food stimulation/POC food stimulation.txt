1) “EM gating” → growth environment control
A) Light as the main growth “field”

Plants respond strongest to light spectrum, intensity, and timing:

red/blue ratios, far-red, and UV-A (careful) can steer morphology

pulsing light (duty cycles) can maintain growth while reducing power

day/night photoperiod control drives flowering and leaf expansion

So your “dual-channel” becomes:

Channel 1: spectral control (LED mix + timing)

Channel 2: intensity modulation/pulsing + feedback (sensors)

B) Electric fields (small, localized) for rooting/ion movement

There’s research into mild electric fields and electroculture effects, but results are mixed. Where it’s most grounded:

nutrient solution management (hydroponics): conductivity/EC, pH control, aeration

preventing biofilm in lines (mostly a sanitation/engineering problem)

2) Plasma tech → sterilization and water/air treatment

This is one of the most practical uses:

cold plasma can inactivate microbes on surfaces, seeds, or packaging (when used correctly)

plasma can generate reactive species (ozone/NOx) so you must manage safety and concentrations

In a grow setup, it could serve as:

periodic air sanitization (in a closed duct, not blasting plants directly)

surface sterilization of tools/trays

possibly seed treatment (some evidence of improved germination in certain conditions)

3) Polymer composite layer → smart greenhouse film / insulation

Your polymer-layer thinking maps cleanly to:

UV/IR selective films (let PAR through, reject unwanted IR to control heat)

insulating layers to stabilize temperature/humidity

embedded sensors or conductive traces (for monitoring, not high power)

4) TPV heat recovery → “waste heat to power” in a grow room

If you have strong thermal gradients (industrial waste heat, high-temp sources), TPV could:

reclaim some power

help balance thermal loads
But for most grow tents/rooms, heat pumps + good insulation are usually more cost-effective than TPV.

A simple PoC you could run (safe and measurable)

Goal: show that “dual-channel control” improves growth efficiency and consistency.

Setup

Two identical grow bins or tents (A/B)

Same cultivar, soil/hydro, nutrients, temperature, humidity, CO₂

A = normal constant-light schedule

B = dual-channel:

spectrum recipe (e.g., veg vs flower)

pulsed intensity schedule with feedback from sensors

Measurements (weekly)

biomass gain, leaf area, internode length

yield (if fruiting), root mass

energy used (kWh)

disease incidence (optional plasma sanitation group)

Success looks like: equal or better growth with lower kWh, or faster growth at same kWh, plus lower mold/biofilm issues if you add plasma sanitation.
