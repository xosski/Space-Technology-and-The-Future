Eden-01 Hydroponics PoC
EM-Quiet Chamber + Optional Stimulus Layers (after baseline)
1) PoC Goal

Demonstrate that an “EM-stabilized” hydroponics chamber improves:

sensor integrity (pH/EC/CO₂/RH noise and drift)

control-loop stability (fewer oscillations, fewer resets)

crop repeatability (consistent time-to-harvest and biomass)

…and only after that, test whether optional stimulus (e.g., plasma-activated water) produces a measurable delta.

2) Build Configuration
Two matched grow bins (A/B)

A (Control): standard hydroponics chamber, normal wiring

B (Eden): same chamber but with:

segmented conductive dispersal liner (“tiling,” not a continuous metal shell)

externalized LED drivers/pump controllers (noisy gear outside grow volume)

star-ground + shielded twisted pair sensor lines

wet/dry separation (electronics isolated from humidity)

Grow method: NFT or drip recirculating (simplest to keep consistent).
Crops (recommended for fast cycles): lettuce, basil, spinach, cilantro.

3) PoC Phases (matches your Eden-01 plan)
Phase 1 — Chamber Integrity + Environmental Stability (72 hours)

Objective: prove you can hold stable conditions without condensation events.

leak test / smoke test

verify temp/RH/CO₂ stability

verify water loop stability (pH/EC control)

Pass criteria:

no uncontrolled condensation

setpoints held in-band continuously

Phase 2 — EM Quiet Validation (Eden differentiator)

Run identical loads and measure noise:

PWM dimming sweeps on LEDs

pump start/stop cycling

dosing pump activation bursts

Measure:

pH sensor variance and spikes

EC variance and spikes

CO₂ sensor noise

comms errors/resets

correlation between switching events and sensor glitches

Pass criteria:

measurable reduction in noise/spikes in B vs A

fewer comm dropouts/resets in B

Phase 3 — Baseline Crop Repeatability (2–3 grow cycles)

Objective: repeatable yields with no stimulus.
Track:

germination rate

time-to-harvest

fresh weight + dry weight

uniformity (std dev of plant mass)

root mass (subset)

contamination rate (biofilm/mold)

Pass criteria:

lower cycle-to-cycle variance in B

equal or improved biomass at same kWh

Phase 4 — Optional Stimulus A/B (only after Phase 3 passes)

This is where your “stimulation” ideas belong, but isolated so they can’t corrupt baseline controls.

Pick one stimulus to test first:

Option 4A — Plasma-Activated Water (PAW) in the recirc loop (experimental)

PAW module operates in a closed side-loop, not in the grow chamber air

dosing is controlled and reversible

Measure:

root health markers

growth rate delta

pathogen/biofilm reduction

stress signs (tip burn, wilting)

Option 4B — Low-field electroculture pattern (very cautious)

low amplitude, localized near root zone

strict A/B tray split

Pass criteria:

repeatable improvement across cycles

no increase in stress or disease

4) Data Logging Schema (minimum)

Log at 1–10 second intervals:

Temp/RH, CO₂, PAR/PPFD

reservoir pH, EC, water temp, water level

pump states, dosing events

LED dimming state, driver temps

alarms, resets, comm errors

5) What this PoC proves

Engineering win: “the machines stop lying” (sensor truthfulness and control stability)

Biology win (optional): stimulus effects only if they survive proper A/B testing

Scale path: modular trays, sealed service boundaries, reliable hydroponics module design
