Addendum: Targeted Tumor Disruption Layer

While the CRΔDL photonic and regenerative stack is well suited for recovery support, calibration, and controlled biological repair, a complete oncology-oriented configuration would likely require an initial targeted tumor-disruption stage before regenerative phases begin.

In this framing, the first stage is not described as a generalized destructive radiation burst, but as a localized debulking layer intended to disrupt, reduce, or sterilize the primary cancer burden in a controlled treatment field. At a high level, this role is conceptually closer to established clinical modalities such as:

external beam radiation therapy, which delivers focused radiation to a defined region of the body,
brachytherapy, in which radiation is placed in or near the tumor,
or thermal ablation methods, which use localized heat to damage tumor tissue in selected cases.

Within the CRΔDL architecture, this disruption layer would be treated as the front-end tumor-reduction phase, while the photonic resonance, metabolic support, regenerative calibration, and cradle-control systems would be positioned as the post-disruption support environment rather than the sole tumor-killing mechanism.

This creates a more coherent therapeutic sequence:

1. Localized tumor disruption
Reduce or damage the dominant cancer cell population through a targeted local treatment field.

2. Residual-field assessment
Use imaging, diagnostics, and calibration layers to determine remaining burden and tissue condition.

3. Photonic / regenerative support phase
Apply CRΔDL-controlled stabilization, monitoring, and recovery-oriented support to surrounding tissue and the treatment environment.

4. Ongoing surveillance
Track recurrence risk, tissue response, and residual disease markers over time.
