Yes — conceptually, that would be a plasma doping / conditioning chamber.

You would not really make plasma “malleable” like clay, but you could change how easily it is shaped by altering:

ion species
electron density
collision rate
conductivity
recombination behavior
temperature profile
magnetic responsiveness

Noble gases are especially useful conceptually because they ionize cleanly and are commonly used in plasma systems. Argon, neon, xenon, etc. could act like different “plasma carrier fluids.”

Resin/polymer is trickier. Passing hot plasma through polymer would usually break it down, outgas it, carbonize it, or contaminate the plasma. But that could still be useful if framed as a feedstock vaporization / plasma chemistry chamber rather than a clean conditioning medium.

Nitrogen/Oxygen	Reactive chemistry
Carbon vapor	Lattice/material feedstock
Polymer vapor	Complex plasma chemistry / carbon-rich feedstock

The realistic framing is: you are tuning plasma composition and electromagnetic response to improve confinement, shaping, and plasmoid formation.
