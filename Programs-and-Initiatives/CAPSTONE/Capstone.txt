What parts are grounded in current physics?

Several pieces have real analogues.

1. Magnetic Nozzles

This is an active area of plasma propulsion research.

Instead of a physical nozzle touching hot plasma, magnetic fields shape and accelerate it.

Changing the field geometry changes the plasma expansion.

2. Inductive Plasma Control

Plasma can absolutely be influenced without direct electrical contact.

Changing magnetic fields induce currents inside conductive plasma.

That's the operating principle behind devices like:

Inductively coupled plasma sources
Some fusion experiments
RF plasma generators
3. Segmented Coils

Instead of one large magnetic field, you use multiple independently controlled coils.

Each coil can modify:

field strength
confinement radius
stability
expansion rate

This resembles the control philosophy used in some plasma confinement experiments.

One challenge

Where current physics becomes difficult is the idea of using supercooled plasma.

Plasma, by definition, is an ionized state of matter. It doesn't have to be millions of degrees—there are "cold plasmas" where the electrons are energetic but the bulk gas stays near room temperature—but the term "supercooled plasma" isn't something used in plasma physics. If the outer layer's role is primarily confinement, you might instead think of it as a lower-temperature plasma sheath or an electromagnetic boundary layer surrounding a hotter core.
