PoC: Lazarus Rescue Hook

A compact EVA recovery system that fires a smart tether hook toward a ship, station, drone, or rescue rail, then uses controlled retraction plus micro plasma-vector bursts to bring a drifting astronaut/object back safely.

Purpose

Recover someone drifting in space without relying only on suit gas. Think “space grappling hook,” but with braking, steering, and emergency auto-return.

Core Components

Smart Grapnel Head
Magnetic clamp, mechanical claw, or hull-safe anchor.

Conductive Tether Line
High-strength cable carrying power, telemetry, and safety status.

Auto-Reel Motor
Retracts the line with controlled acceleration.

Plasma Vector Collar
Small directional micro-bursts for steering, braking, and rotation control.

Deadman Recall System
Automatically reels the user back if vitals, motion, or manual control fail.

Collision Avoidance Logic
Slows approach so the user does not become a holy orbital bowling ball.

Operating Sequence
Astronaut drifts outside safe range.
Lazarus Hook targets nearest safe anchor.
Grapnel fires and attaches.
System confirms lock strength.
Auto-reel begins low-force retrieval.
Plasma collar fires correction bursts to stabilize spin.
Approach speed is reduced near the ship.
Final docking grip engages.
Movement Modes

Recall Mode — returns user to ship.
Swing Mode — redirects around debris.
Brake Mode — slows dangerous approach.
Anchor Mode — holds position outside hull.
Deadman Mode — automatic recovery.

Testable Bench Version

Use:

small CO₂/gas thrusters instead of plasma,
fishing-line style tether,
magnetic clamp,
air-bearing puck or low-friction cart,
Arduino/Raspberry Pi controller.

Success = controlled pullback, reduced spin, and safe stop near target.

Main Limitation

The hook must anchor to something. Without an anchor or expelled mass, it cannot move the user. Physics is rude but consistent.

LAZRUK System Overview

The LAZRUK is a compact EVA rescue and maneuver system designed for astronauts, maintenance crews, and emergency recovery teams operating outside spacecraft.

Unlike conventional EVA thruster packs that rely entirely on stored propellant, the LAZRUK combines:

Smart tether technology
Autonomous retrieval logic
Plasma-assisted vector correction
Emergency recovery protocols

into a single wearable system.
