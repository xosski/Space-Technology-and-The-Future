# PALMS

## Plasma Aligned Layered Matter Synthesizer

### Proof of Concept (POC)

**Version 0.1 – Conceptual Architecture**

---

# Abstract

The **Plasma Aligned Layered Matter Synthesizer (PALMS)** is a conceptual high-energy materials processing system intended for closed-loop resource recovery in long-duration space missions. Rather than viewing waste as disposable material, PALMS treats all matter as recoverable atomic feedstock through controlled plasma processing and electromagnetic separation.

The concept integrates programmable plasma confinement, layered electromagnetic fields, and staged thermal processing to deconstruct complex materials into elemental or molecular constituents that may be recycled by onboard manufacturing systems.

PALMS is envisioned as a companion technology to the **PRISM Rail (Plasma-Routed Integrated Shipwide Manifold)** and **MFHAP (Matter Foundational Hex Atomization Platform)** within a speculative closed-loop spacecraft architecture.

---

# 1. Mission Statement

Modern spacecraft carry finite resources.

Every kilogram launched represents cost, energy, and mission duration.

PALMS proposes a conceptual system where nearly every solid or liquid waste stream becomes recoverable material rather than discarded mass.

The guiding philosophy is simple:

> **Matter is never waste. Only misplaced resources.**

# 2. System Overview

PALMS consists of five functional layers.

```
Waste Intake
      │
      ▼
Alignment Chamber
      │
      ▼
Layered Plasma Reactor
      │
      ▼
Electromagnetic Separation
      │
      ▼
Recovered Material Storage
```

Each stage performs a distinct processing function while remaining electrically and thermally isolated from adjacent systems.

---

# 3. Operating Principle

Incoming material is introduced into a sealed processing chamber.

Successive plasma layers progressively increase energy density while electromagnetic confinement maintains stability.

The objective is not "destroying" matter.

Instead, the system seeks to reduce complex molecular structures into simpler chemical species, ions, or atoms that can be sorted and recovered.

Conceptually:

```
Waste
↓

Heating

↓

Pyrolysis

↓

Gasification

↓

Ionization

↓

Electromagnetic Separation

↓

Reusable Elements
```

---

# 4. Layered Plasma Architecture

Unlike conventional plasma torches, PALMS proposes multiple plasma regions operating at different temperatures and field strengths.

### Layer 1 — Alignment Plasma

Purpose:

• Preheats material

• Removes volatile compounds

• Stabilizes entry conditions

---

### Layer 2 — Dissociation Plasma

Purpose:

• Break molecular bonds

• Convert solids into gaseous products

• Initiate ion formation

---

### Layer 3 — Ionization Zone

Purpose:

Increase ion concentration for electromagnetic manipulation.

---

### Layer 4 — Separation Region

Electromagnetic fields conceptually separate particles according to properties such as charge-to-mass ratio, similar in principle to existing plasma processing and mass separation techniques.

---

### Layer 5 — Collection Zone

Recovered materials are directed toward storage reservoirs for future use.

Potential outputs include:

• Carbon

• Hydrogen

• Oxygen

• Nitrogen

• Metallic particles

• Water vapor

• Mineral compounds

---

# 5. Geometric Plasma Control

PALMS adopts a speculative geometric confinement approach inspired by programmable electromagnetic lattices.

Rather than maintaining a single cylindrical plasma channel, surrounding magnetic fields are divided into controllable cells.

Illustrative geometry:

```
◇ ◇ ◇ ◇

⬡ ⬡ ⬡

◇ ◇ ◇ ◇
```

Each cell may independently adjust:

* Magnetic field strength
* Plasma density
* Confinement geometry
* Localized heating
* Flow direction

The intent is to improve stability and enable adaptive control rather than relying on a uniform field.

# 8. Proposed Development Path

## Phase I

Bench-scale plasma confinement.

Objective:

Demonstrate stable layered plasma regions.

---

## Phase II

Material decomposition.

Objective:

Evaluate conversion of representative waste into simpler chemical products.

---

## Phase III

Electromagnetic separation.

Objective:

Investigate recovery of selected ions or elemental fractions from processed material.

---

## Phase IV

Thermal integration.

Objective:

Recover usable heat for spacecraft systems.

---

## Phase V

Closed-loop demonstration.

Objective:

Operate PALMS with upstream waste handling and downstream manufacturing simulators.

# 11. Conclusion

PALMS is presented as a speculative conceptual architecture for closed-loop material recovery. Rather than viewing spacecraft waste as a disposal problem, it reframes waste as a recoverable inventory of chemical elements.

Within the broader conceptual ecosystem:

* **PRISM Rail** distributes power, heat, and services.
* **PALMS** recovers elemental feedstock from waste streams.
* **MFHAP** represents a downstream manufacturing concept that could utilize recovered materials.

Together, these concepts illustrate a vision of an integrated spacecraft infrastructure in which energy and matter circulate through a unified resource management system. Significant scientific and engineering advances would be required before any such architecture could move beyond the conceptual stage.
