Addendum B — Plasma-Burst Active Confirmation Layer
Concept

After passive detection identifies a likely vessel by background attenuation, IR contrast, or EM anomaly, the system emits a short, directed burst of low-temperature ionized gas/plasma along the suspected bearing.

The burst interacts with local electromagnetic fields, charged particles, and reflected energy signatures. Sensors then measure the return disturbance to refine target location, velocity, and classification.

Hypothesis

If passive sensors detect an anomalous energy signature, then a controlled ionized burst directed toward that anomaly may produce measurable scattering, reflection, or field distortion that improves target confirmation.

Function
passive detection
→ estimated bearing
→ short plasma/ionized-gas burst
→ burst interacts with target field/signature
→ sensors observe scattering/phase disturbance
→ target confidence increases
Why Use It

Possible benefits:

creates a temporary active sensing medium
may enhance weak EM reflections
can probe charged or magnetic environments
may reveal artificial field structures
can be fired only after passive detection, reducing exposure time
What It Measures
plasma scattering
phase shift
recombination glow
magnetic deflection
charge interaction
Doppler-like movement of disturbed plasma
delayed return emissions
Limits

This would not be stealth-perfect. A plasma burst may be visible in IR, RF, optical, or particle sensors.

Main issues:

energy cost
plume detectability
plasma dispersion
short useful lifetime
beam containment
false returns from natural charged particles
poor performance over very long distances
Safer Lab Prototype

Use substitutes first:

fog/smoke plume with laser sheet
low-energy ionized gas tube
plasma globe-style discharge
UV/IR camera
magnetometer
photodiodes

Goal: prove that a controlled “active medium” improves detection of an object already found passively.

Best Use Case

Not constant scanning.

Use it as a confirmation ping:

Listen silently.
Detect shadow.
Whisper plasma.
Watch how the ghost bends.

This makes the plasma system a final-stage verifier, not the primary radar.
