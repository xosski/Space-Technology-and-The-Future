System Overview
Phase 1: Atomized Solution Pre-Wash

Ultra-fine mist of CO2/H2O or enzymatic solution coats the body.

Begins molecular loosening of dirt, oil, and microfilm.

May optionally be infused with biodegradable nanosoaps or surfactants.

Phase 2: UV-C Light Sweep

High-frequency ultraviolet light sweeps over user.

Targets bacterial and viral structures.

Also serves to photo-activate surface layers for plasma receptivity.

Phase 3: Noble Gas Cold Plasma Exposure

Plasma is generated using noble gases (Neon, Argon, Xenon).

Low-temperature discharge cleans at the microscopic level.

Breaks down biological residue and denatures toxins.

Gas Options:

Neon: Best for rapid surface ionization.

Argon: Balanced for all-surface neutralization.

Xenon: Deeper penetrating, ideal for heavy contamination.

Phase 4: High-Pressure Air Dry

High-velocity air removes loose particulates and dried matter.

May be ionized to reduce static buildup.

Phase 5: UV-C Confirmation Sweep

Final sweep ensures surface sterility post-plasma.

Optional biomarker verification through fluorescence detection.
