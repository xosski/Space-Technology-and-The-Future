**Title:**
Unified Magnetohydrodynamic Cooling and Shielding System for Compact High-Energy Reactors (GhostCore POC)

**Abstract:**
This white paper presents a novel approach to cooling and radiation shielding in high-energy reactor systems through the integration of a molten metal magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) loop. Leveraging superconducting confinement, this design enables pump-free circulation of a lead-based liquid metal coolant, offering combined functions of thermal regulation, electromagnetic field control, and radiation protection. This unified system is ideal for compact, high-output energy sources such as the GhostCore reactor and other advanced propulsion or space-based energy systems.

---

**1. Introduction**
Advanced energy systems demand innovative thermal and radiation management. Traditional methods treat cooling, shielding, and magnetic field control as discrete subsystems. We propose a unified design that merges these into a single fluidic loop using molten lead or Pb-Bi eutectic, confined and controlled via superconducting materials.

This system forms an MHD heat pipe driven by EM fields, removing the need for mechanical pumps and enabling modular, distributed, and dynamic control of thermal and shielding characteristics.

**2. System Components and Functions**

**2.1. Molten Lead Loop**

* **Coolant:** Lead or Pb-Bi eutectic for high thermal mass and neutron absorption.
* **Conductivity:** Enables Lorentz-force propulsion via EM fields.
* **Shielding:** Dense medium absorbs gamma and neutron radiation.

**2.2. Superconducting Shell**

* **Material:** Type-II superconductors (e.g. YBCO, MgB₂).
* **Meissner Effect:** Flux exclusion ensures magnetic confinement.
* **Flux Pinning:** Enables field anchoring and flow guidance.

**2.3. EM Field Coils**

* **Function:** Generate directional Lorentz force for flow control.
* **Precision:** Enable modulation of velocity, direction, and thermal gradients.

**3. Thermo-MHD Operation**

**3.1. Flow Induction**
Lorentz force:
[\vec{F} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})]
Drives self-sustaining liquid flow within superconducting confines.

**3.2. Thermal Exchange**
Heat from the GhostCore reactor is absorbed and transported to radiative fins or secondary coolant exchangers without the use of mechanical impellers.

**3.3. Shielding**
The flowing metal loop serves as a dynamic radiation barrier, particularly effective against high-energy particles.
