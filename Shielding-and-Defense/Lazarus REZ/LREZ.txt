What it could do

A top/bottom Lazarus capacitor array could support:

- emergency shield bloom
- high-energy plasma/EM dispersal
- incoming projectile deflection
- overload dumping away from the hull
- “flash” discharge to break target lock
- directional pulse defense
- capacitor-fed Angel Drift evasive burst
- emergency braking or spin-state correction

The top and bottom placement matters because it gives the ship biaxial discharge symmetry. Instead of dumping energy from one side and twisting the ship apart, the arrays can fire in balanced pairs.

Top array fires upward
Bottom array fires downward
Net translation ≈ low
Defensive field expansion ≈ high

Or, if you deliberately offset timing:

Top fires first
Bottom fires delayed
Result = controlled roll / evasive torque

That connects directly to the SCDE / Lazarus torque concept, where counter-polarized plasma-electric divergence is used to create spin-state induction and torque behavior rather than ordinary linear thrust.

Main operating modes
1. Aegis Bloom

Both arrays discharge outward at the same time.

Purpose:
expand a temporary EM/plasma shield bubble

Best for missiles, debris, radiation bursts, or incoming energy weapons.

2. Dispersal Curtain

The arrays discharge in staggered pulses.

Purpose:
create layered defensive interference

Think of it like throwing sheets of charged plasma/EM noise around the hull.

3. Overload Vent

The ship dumps excess capacitor or reactor energy away from the core.

Purpose:
prevent internal arcing or bus failure

This is the “don’t become a grenade with hallways” mode.

4. Spin Assist / Angel Drift Coupling

Top and bottom arrays discharge with asymmetric timing.

Purpose:
create roll torque or evasive vector shift

This would pair well with your Spectral Divergent Drives.

5. Blackout Recovery Pulse

The Elohim lattice feeds enough reserve into the Lazarus arrays to restart shield buses, field coils, or damaged drive control.

Purpose:
bring critical systems back online after impact or EMP-like failure
Key design rule

The capacitor arrays should not be treated like normal batteries.

They are pulse organs.

Battery = slow storage
Lazarus capacitor = violent memory
Elohim lattice = distributed safety net
Aegis skin = field-shaping surface

The biggest danger is uncontrolled discharge. If the top array fires harder than the bottom, the ship can experience unwanted torque, hull stress, field shear, or internal bus rebound.

So the system needs:

- synchronized discharge gates
- capacitor phase lock
- thermal dump channels
- passive bleed-off paths
- top/bottom balance sensors
- hull torsion monitoring
- Elohim surge absorption reserve
