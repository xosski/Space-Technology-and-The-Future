POC Name: Ferroplasma Reactive Armor

Concept:
A layered armor system that uses conductive metal, ferromagnetic control layers, electromagnetic fields, and low-temperature plasma channels to redirect heat, disperse electrical energy, and reduce thermal damage.

Core Hypothesis

If plasma or ionized gas is routed through insulated microchannels beneath external armor plating, and that layer is controlled by electromagnetic pulses, then incoming heat or charged energy can be spread, redirected, or partially absorbed before reaching the protected structure.

Basic Layer Stack
[Outer Impact Plate]
Hardened steel / tungsten alloy / titanium alloy

[Conductive Spread Layer]
Copper, aluminum, graphene-like conductor, or thermal lattice

[Ferromagnetic Control Layer]
Soft iron, silicon steel, ferrite composite

[Insulated Plasma Channels]
Ceramic-lined microtubes carrying ionized gas / low-temperature plasma

[Cooling / Heat Sink Layer]
Liquid cooling, phase-change material, or heat pipe network

[Inner Structural Shell]
Composite armor, ceramic backing, or vehicle hull
How It Works

When heat, plasma, electricity, or directed energy hits the armor:

impact detected
→ sensors trigger local electromagnetic pulse
→ ferromagnetic layer magnetizes
→ plasma channel becomes field-guided
→ heat/electrical energy spreads across conductive lattice
→ cooling layer absorbs excess energy
→ armor resets

The goal is not to make the armor “invincible.”
The goal is to buy time, reduce peak heat transfer, and prevent one point from taking all the damage.

Prototype Version

A safe lab-scale version would not use dangerous plasma first.

Phase 1: Thermal Proof

Use:

metal plate
+ copper heat spreader
+ soft iron layer
+ ceramic insulated channel
+ liquid cooling loop
+ temperature sensors

Test against controlled heat sources:

heat gun
soldering iron
small torch at safe distance
IR lamp

Measure:

front plate temperature
back plate temperature
cooldown time
thermal spread pattern

Success condition:

back surface stays cooler than control plate
heat spreads instead of forming one hot spot
cooldown improves
Phase 2: Electromagnetic Control

Add:

copper coil around ferromagnetic layer
low-voltage pulsed current
Hall sensor
thermal sensor array
microcontroller

Test whether pulsed fields improve heat distribution.

Phase 3: Ionized Gas / Plasma Substitute

Instead of real plasma at first, use:

argon or neon gas tube
plasma globe-style low-energy discharge
sealed glass/ceramic test cell

This proves field interaction without building a dangerous plasma weapon.
