2. Problem Statement

Escape systems for high-risk platforms (orbital habitats, deep-space craft, high-altitude vehicles, or hostile-environment facilities) must provide:

Rapid egress under catastrophic failure

Repeatable reliability (low moving parts, high tolerance to damage)

Minimal mass/volume penalty

Non-explosive operation preferred in oxygen-rich or fragile hull environments

Conventional options (solid rockets, compressed gas, explosive bolts, mechanical rails) trade speed for hazard, complexity, or maintenance. A field-driven acceleration corridor offers a route to rapid ejection with lower chemical risk, provided it can be made stable and survivable.
