1. Core Concept

The FTG Skip-Anchor Traversal System replaces black-hole-based traversal with deployable electromagnetic phase-anchor drones.

Instead of entering a singularity, the ship projects or launches a drone ahead of its flight path. The drone generates a temporary EM/plasma field bubble. As the ship’s dual oscillating shield fields intersect that bubble, the drone collapses its field at a precise phase interval.

That collapse creates a short-lived traversal seam — a “micro-warp skip bubble” — allowing the vessel to jump forward in discrete pulses.

The ship does not continuously exceed light speed.

It skips across compressed traversal states.

Like a stone across water.

2. Existing Foundation

This builds directly from the EM Phase-Cancellation Anchor idea, where opposing EM waveforms collapse a stabilized field shell and briefly create a fold-compatible anchor without requiring the vessel to enter a black hole.

It also replaces the older black-hole FTG model, which treated singularities as transit tunnels and tactical staging grounds.

Project Revenant still provides the original doctrine: black holes were treated as gravitational infrastructure, but this new version miniaturizes the “anchor” function into a drone-based field event.
