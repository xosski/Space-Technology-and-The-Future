Proof of Concept: Ghoststep Helix Plasma Vector System
1. Purpose

A maneuver system that stores ionized plasma inside a magnetic containment chamber, then releases controlled bursts through helical vector nozzles to create rapid lateral ship movement.

This is not “free movement.” It works only if plasma/charged mass is expelled or pushed against an external field. Fully trapped internal plasma would not move the ship.

2. Core Hypothesis

A magnetically confined plasma charge, released through a helical EM-guided nozzle, can produce short-duration directional impulse useful for emergency sidestep maneuvers.

The system pairs well with your WraithHalo Maglev Shield Ring, which already acts as an outer field-shaping layer around the vessel.

3. System Components

A. Plasma Containment Bottle
Magnetic chamber holding ionized plasma until discharge.

B. Helix Vector Nozzles
Angled output channels wrapped in rotating EM fields to shape the plasma into a tight spiral stream.

C. Maglev Engine Housing
Magnetic engine core that stabilizes the plasma before release.

D. Burst Capacitor Bank
Stores charge for emergency impulse firing.

E. WraithHalo Sync Layer
Coordinates shield-field opening so the burst exits cleanly without destabilizing the outer EM barrier.

F. Targeting / Threat Sensor
Detects incoming projectile path and calculates dodge vector.

4. Operating Sequence
Threat detected.
Ship computes minimum escape vector.
WraithHalo opens a temporary field window.
Plasma bottle routes charge to the correct side nozzle.
Helix nozzle spins/compresses plasma output.
Plasma burst exits.
Ship receives opposite-direction impulse.
Shield ring recloses and absorbs field turbulence.
5. Maneuver Modes

Ghoststep — short lateral dodge burst.
Helix Kick — stronger directional impulse.
Shard Slip — micro-adjustment for avoiding debris.
Aegis Pulse — defensive plasma dump into shield field.
Emergency Bloom — uncontrolled overload dump away from hull.

6. Testable POC Path

Phase 1 — Simulation
Model ship mass, plasma exhaust velocity, burst duration, and lateral displacement.

Phase 2 — Bench Plasma Jet
Use a safe commercial plasma source to test EM shaping and helical output behavior.

Phase 3 — Magnetic Nozzle Demo
Test whether coil geometry can collimate or redirect ionized gas/plasma.

Phase 4 — Small Reaction Cart Test
Mount a safe compressed-gas or ion-thruster analog on an air track/cart and prove impulse-based sidestep movement.

Phase 5 — Shield Integration Model
Simulate interaction between plasma burst and outer EM ring.

7. Success Metrics
Measurable lateral impulse.
Repeatable burst direction.
Stable magnetic containment before release.
Helical output remains coherent longer than straight output.
Shield field disruption stays below safe threshold.
Burst timing is fast enough for evasive correction.
8. Limitations

This will not make a large ship dodge bullets like anime nonsense unless the impulse is enormous. The real limits are ship mass, reaction time, acceleration tolerance, heat, and available stored energy.

Best use case: last-second course correction, not full evasive teleportation.

9. Concept Summary

The Ghoststep Helix Plasma Vector System is an emergency plasma impulse maneuver protocol. It stores charged plasma, shapes it with helical EM fields, releases it through vector nozzles, and uses the resulting reaction force to shove the vessel out of a projected impact path.
