Eden Chamber
Proof-of-Concept (POC) White Paper — EM-Stabilized Controlled-Environment Grow Module (Eden-01)
Version

POC v0.1 (architecture + test plan)

Abstract

Eden-01 is a controlled-environment grow chamber designed to be stable, modular, and “electromagnetically quiet.” It repurposes EM-field management concepts (segmented dispersal liners, controlled inductive routing, and zoned shielding) to create a protected inner habitat where lighting, pumps, sensors, and automation operate with reduced interference. The chamber’s first goal is reliability and repeatable crop output; optional EM “stimulus modes” (electroculture, pulsed fields, plasma-activated water) are treated as experimental layers only after baseline performance is validated.

1) Goals and Non-Goals
Primary Goals (POC Success)

Environmental stability: tight control of temperature, humidity, CO₂, airflow, and nutrient delivery.

Instrumentation integrity: low sensor noise, low comms dropouts, high repeatability run-to-run.

Modularity: tray-level replaceability and sealed service boundaries.

EM hygiene: reduced EMI coupling between high-power subsystems (LED drivers, pumps) and sensitive sensing/control.

Non-Goals (for v0.1)

Not claiming guaranteed yield boosts from EM stimulus.

Not building a full commercial vertical farm—this is a repeatable module.

Not relying on exotic cooling; conventional thermal management is preferred for POC.

2) System Overview
2.1 Layered Architecture (Outside → Inside)

Layer A — Infrastructure Shell

Power conversion, lighting drivers, pump controllers, compressors/dehumidifiers (as needed)

Designed to be “noisy by nature,” physically separated

Layer B — EM Dispersal / Shield Liner

Segmented conductive liner cells to disrupt large eddy-current loops and reduce resonance coupling

Optional lossy/absorptive inserts (ferrite-style tiles, resistive films) to damp remaining modes

Purpose: keep the inner volume “quiet” and prevent the shell from becoming an antenna

Layer C — Eden Habitat Volume

Sealed grow space: plants, sensors, air distribution, lighting field

Designed as the “clean room” layer

2.2 Core Subsystems

Air & Atmosphere

Temp control: heater + cooling coil (or Peltier only for small volumes)

Humidity: dehumidifier coil and/or desiccant loop

CO₂ injection (food-safe) + mixing fan

HEPA + carbon filtration (optional but recommended)

Lighting

High-efficiency LED array with dimming control

Drivers physically placed in Layer A; only DC power enters Layer C

Hydroponics / Nutrient Delivery

Recirculating reservoir (outside the habitat if possible)

Per-tray nutrient film (NFT), drip, or aeroponics manifold

Sensors: pH, EC, water temp, reservoir level, flow

Sensing & Control

Habitat: temp/RH, CO₂, light intensity (PAR), leaf temp (IR optional)

Water: pH/EC/temp, flow, leak detection

Control: microcontroller/PLC + data logging + alarms

Optional Contactless Power (Inductive Tray Power)

Inductive power pads to energize tray modules without corroding connectors

POC option only after baseline chamber stability is proven

Optional Sterilization / Water Treatment

UV-C sterilization in recirculation loop

Plasma-activated water module (experimental; off by default)

3) Eden-01 Physical Form Factor (POC Target)

Internal grow volume: ~0.5–2.0 m³ (bench-scale)

Trays: 2–6 modular trays (stacked or single plane)

Serviceability: front door access + slide-out tray rails

Separation: “dry electronics zone” physically isolated from “wet grow zone”

4) EM Hygiene Strategy (The “Eden” Twist)
4.1 Why EM Management Matters Here

In dense controlled-environment builds, failures often come from:

sensor drift and noise

comms resets

ground loops and moisture-corroded connectors

EMI from LED drivers and motors coupling into analog sensors

Eden-01 treats EM stability as a first-class design constraint.

4.2 Dispersal Liner Concepts (POC Implementation)

Build the liner as segmented conductive cells (think “tiling,” not a continuous metal cylinder).

Avoid long continuous loops; use breaks/slots to prevent resonant currents.

Add optional absorptive zones in corners and around feedthroughs.

4.3 Wiring Rules (Hard Requirements)

Drivers and switching power supplies stay outside Layer C.

Only low-noise DC and comms pass through feedthroughs.

Star-ground topology with controlled bonding points.

Shielded twisted pairs for sensor lines; isolate analog from PWM motor wiring.

5) Operating Modes
Mode 0 — Baseline Grow (required)

Standard horticulture control loops only (temp/RH/CO₂/light/nutrient)

EM stimulus features disabled

This mode is used to establish repeatability and crop benchmarks

Mode 1 — EM Quiet Hardening (POC focus)

Dispersal liner enabled + cable routing discipline enforced

Evaluate reduction in noise, dropouts, and drift

Mode 2 — Experimental Stimulus (optional, after Mode 0/1 success)

Low-field stimulus patterns

Water treatment experiments (e.g., plasma-activated water) as a controlled A/B test

Must be isolated so it can’t corrupt baseline controls

6) Control Targets (POC Suggested Setpoints)

(These are placeholders; choose based on crop species.)

Temp: 20–26°C

RH: 50–70% with VPD-based control

CO₂: 800–1200 ppm (when lights on)

Photoperiod: 16/8 or crop-specific

Nutrient: crop-specific EC and pH bands

7) Risks and Mitigations
7.1 Condensation + Electronics

Risk: moisture causes shorts, corrosion, sensor drift

Mitigation: strict wet/dry separation, sealed feedthroughs, conformal coating, drip loops

7.2 EMI Coupling into Sensors

Risk: unstable readings cause control oscillations

Mitigation: dispersal liner, driver relocation, shielding, star-ground, filtering

7.3 Pathogens / Biofilm in Recirculation

Risk: crop loss

Mitigation: UV loop, filtration, periodic sanitation cycle, reservoir isolation

7.4 Over-automation Instability

Risk: oscillatory climate control

Mitigation: slow control loops, hysteresis bands, rate limits, prioritized constraints (e.g., “no condensation”)

8) POC Test Plan
Phase 1 — Chamber Integrity + Baseline Environment

Objective: prove you can hold stable conditions.

Leak test habitat (pressure decay / smoke test)

Validate temp/RH/CO₂ stability over 24–72 hrs

Validate water loop: stable pH/EC control in recirculation

Pass criteria

No uncontrolled condensation events

Control holds within acceptable bands for a full run window

Phase 2 — EM Quiet Validation (Eden Differentiator)

Objective: prove the dispersal liner + layout reduces operational noise.
Test with identical operation under:

Configuration A: no liner

Configuration B: segmented liner

Configuration C: liner + absorptive damping

Measure:

sensor noise (variance, spikes)

comm errors/reset counts

correlated noise during PWM dimming / motor starts

Pass criteria

measurable reduction in sensor noise and event spikes

improved comm stability under worst-case switching loads

Phase 3 — Crop Baseline Repeatability

Objective: repeatable yields without stimulus.

Grow the same cultivar across at least 2–3 cycles

Record germination rate, time-to-harvest, biomass, uniformity, contamination rate

Pass criteria

cycle-to-cycle variance decreases vs a non-hardened control chamber

Phase 4 — Optional Stimulus A/B Tests (Only After Baseline)

Objective: test stimulus without corrupting controls.

Split trays: stimulus ON vs OFF

Everything else identical (light, CO₂, nutrients, VPD)

Track statistical significance across cycles

Pass criteria

any improvement must be repeatable, not a one-off

no increased stress markers (wilting, tip burn, disease)

11) Summary

Eden-01 is a stability-first grow chamber that uses EM-field management and modular zoning to make controlled-environment agriculture more reliable and repeatable. Its “Eden” quality is not magic growth beams—it’s a habitat where the machines stop lying, the sensors stop hallucinating, and the plants get a consistent reality to thrive in.
