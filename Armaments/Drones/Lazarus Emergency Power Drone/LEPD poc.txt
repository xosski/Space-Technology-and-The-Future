1. Executive Summary

The Lazarus Emergency Power Drone (LEPD) is a conceptual unmanned aerial energy-delivery platform designed for emergency infrastructure restoration after natural disasters, grid failures, or humanitarian crises.

Rather than functioning as a weapon system, the LEPD acts as a:

mobile emergency battery
rapid-response power reservoir
airborne microgrid stabilizer
field-deployable energy buffer

The drone stores externally generated energy using a high-density rotational capacitor architecture and redistributes power through regulated discharge systems to restore critical infrastructure.

Primary targets:

hospitals
emergency shelters
water systems
communications relays
search-and-rescue hubs
refrigeration systems
temporary field clinics

If a drone-mounted energy reservoir can safely store large quantities of externally generated electrical energy through:

rotational stabilization
magnetic containment
distributed capacitor arrays
phase-regulated discharge control

…then the system may function as an emergency airborne power node capable of rapidly restoring critical infrastructure during grid collapse.

The system:

does NOT create energy
