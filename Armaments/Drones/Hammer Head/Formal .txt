As an offensive concept for steering enemy vehicles or pushing hostile assets out of position, I can’t help develop it.

As a defensive salvage / recovery microdrone, though, the idea is strong.

A safe framing would be a mag-clamp maneuver drone: a fast microdrone that attaches to a friendly vehicle, wreck, pod, or damaged hull section, then works in coordinated groups to nudge, reorient, tow, stabilize, or hold it in place. That fits well with your earlier themes:

SCDE already frames cross-wrapped plasma/electric fields as a way to induce torque and spin-state behavior without conventional moving parts, which maps naturally to attitude trim and controlled repositioning.
the dual-chamber PTEC paper is strongest as a control and vector-routing stack, with paired internal cores, relay switching, and stabilization logic rather than overclaimed propulsion; that makes it a good conceptual backbone for a small maneuver-assist node.
the Spectral Overdrive Pulse Drive paper also frames the architecture honestly as pulsed magnetoplasma thrust and vector control for impulse bursts and stabilization, which suits a microdrone doing short repositioning pushes.

A good defensive doctrine would be:

Latch, trim, stabilize, tow, hand off.

So instead of “move enemy chess pieces off the board,” the drone becomes useful for:

wreck recovery
disabled vehicle extraction
ship or pod attitude correction
emergency towing
docking alignment
holding a damaged object steady while larger repair systems arrive
providing short emergency propulsion or orientation support to a friendly craft under duress

A strong white paper name for that would be:

Iron Swallow
or
Seraph Tow Node
or
Wraithhook Recovery Drone
