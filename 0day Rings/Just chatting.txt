5) The three design constraints you must bake in early
A) Eddy-current heating

Alternating fields near conductive armor = heat.
If you don’t manage this, your “propulsion skin” becomes a toaster.

Mitigation: segmentation, superconducting traces where possible, ferrite/flux guides, and strict frequency bands.

B) Mechanical stress in the hull

Strong changing fields = forces.
Your outer armor becomes an actuator, and the ship becomes a vibrating instrument.

Mitigation: inner shield as a stiff reference frame + active damping modes.

C) Quench safety

Superconductors fail fast if pushed wrong.
A quench during launch = instant thermal + magnetic chaos.

Mitigation: quench detection, dump resistors, field ramp limits, and “abort-to-capture” where the ring can re-stabilize you.
