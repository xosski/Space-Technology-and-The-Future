1. Executive Summary

The Counter-Rotating Prismatic Field Inverter is a control component mounted at the end of a rotating plasma-field cylinder. Its purpose is to convert stored circular field energy into controlled discharge energy.

The main cylinder stores energy through:

- plasma motion
- magnetic confinement
- electric field pressure
- rotation / flywheel behavior
- thermal plasma energy

The inverter prism sits at one end of the cylinder and spins in the opposite direction while carrying opposite electromagnetic polarity. This creates a controlled boundary layer where the rotating plasma-field flow can be slowed, compressed, phase-shifted, redirected, or extracted.

Doctrine version:

The cylinder stores the storm.
The prism argues with it.
The seam between them becomes the valve.
2. Core Hypothesis

If a rotating plasma-field cylinder is coupled to an opposite-spinning, opposite-polarity prism field element, then the interface between the two may act as a controllable field inversion throat.

That throat could:

- brake rotating plasma-field motion
- compress magnetic field lines
- create controlled shear
- split flow into phase channels
- shape pulse discharge
- regulate emergency energy dumping
- reduce runaway single-mode resonance

The inverter does not create energy. It controls how stored energy exits.

4. Main Components
4.1 Rotating Plasma Cylinder

The main cylinder contains the stored energy loop. It may be modeled as a magnetically confined plasma channel with rotational symmetry.

Its job:

- maintain circulating plasma
- store magnetic and thermal energy
- preserve field coherence
- keep energy away from chamber walls
4.2 Counter-Rotating Prism

The prism spins opposite the cylinder.

Its job:

- break field lock-in
- impose phase inversion
- create controlled shear
- split discharge into predictable paths
- serve as a magnetic brake

The prism can be triangular, square, hexagonal, or more complex.

Triangular prism = 3 discharge phases
Square prism = 4 discharge phases
Hexagonal prism = 6 smoother discharge phases
4.3 Opposite Polarity Field Layer

The prism is electrically or magnetically biased opposite the main cylinder.

This creates extraction pressure at the interface.

Cylinder field: clockwise / positive / storage bias
Prism field: counterclockwise / negative / extraction bias

This does not increase stored energy. It creates a gradient that helps move stored energy out of circulation.

4.4 Shear Interface

This is the most important region.

The shear interface is where the cylinder’s rotating field meets the prism’s counter-rotating field.

Possible effects:

- magnetic compression
- plasma braking
- reconnection-like release
- charge separation
- localized heating
- field-line folding
- resonant phase splitting

This interface must be tightly controlled. It is useful because it is unstable. It is dangerous for the same reason. Charming little monster.

4.5 Discharge Gate

The discharge gate receives the shaped output.

Possible outputs:

- electrical surge
- magnetic pulse
- plasma pulse
- shield recharge burst
- emergency dump into radiators
- controlled thrust-assist impulse
5. Operating Modes
Mode 1 — Passive Brake

The prism spins opposite the cylinder at low field strength.

Purpose:

- gradually slow the storage loop
- recover some rotational energy
- reduce plasma pressure
- prevent runaway resonance
Mode 2 — Phase Inversion

The prism field is strengthened until it creates a stable phase boundary.

Purpose:

- invert polarity at the interface
- split flow into phase channels
- prepare for controlled discharge
Mode 3 — Pulse Shaper

The prism indexes discharge through its geometry.

Purpose:

- turn one chaotic dump into sequential pulses
- smooth the output waveform
- route energy toward a specific system

Example:

hexagonal prism:
pulse 1 → coil bank A
pulse 2 → shield grid
pulse 3 → plasma gate
pulse 4 → radiator dump
pulse 5 → coil bank B
pulse 6 → neutral bleed
Mode 4 — Emergency Dump

The prism becomes a sacrificial field valve.

Purpose:

- rapidly remove stored energy
- prevent cylinder rupture
- protect the central system

Risk:

This mode may damage the prism, discharge gate, or containment coils.
Mode 5 — Lazarus Rephase

The prism synchronizes gradually with the cylinder after instability.

Purpose:

- calm turbulent plasma
- reduce shear
- restore storage mode
- prevent hard shutdown

7. Mathematical Sketch

Total stored energy in the main capacitor:

E_total =
E_rotation
+ E_magnetic
+ E_electric
+ E_plasma
- E_losses

The inverter does not add to E_total. It controls the rate and shape of extraction:

P_out = η × f(shear, polarity_gradient, phase_alignment, gate_coupling)

Where:

P_out = output power
η = efficiency
shear = counter-rotation strength
polarity_gradient = field difference across interface
phase_alignment = timing between cylinder and prism
gate_coupling = how well output is routed

A simplified control variable:

inversion_strength =
spin_difference × polarity_difference × phase_lock_quality

Where:

spin_difference = |ω_cylinder - ω_prism|

Higher inversion strength means faster extraction, but also higher instability risk.

8. Control Algorithm
def counter_rotating_prism_step(cylinder, prism, gate, demand):
    """
    Speculative control loop for a Counter-Rotating Prismatic Field Inverter.
    """

    # 1. Read system state
    spin_delta = abs(cylinder.angular_velocity - prism.angular_velocity)
    polarity_gradient = abs(cylinder.field_polarity - prism.field_polarity)
    plasma_pressure = cylinder.plasma_pressure
    wall_heat = cylinder.wall_temperature
    phase_error = measure_phase_error(cylinder.field_phase, prism.field_phase)

    # 2. Estimate useful inversion force
    inversion_strength = (
        spin_delta
        * polarity_gradient
        * max(0.0, 1.0 - phase_error)
    )

    # 3. Estimate risk
    instability_risk = estimate_instability(
        inversion_strength=inversion_strength,
        plasma_pressure=plasma_pressure,
        wall_heat=wall_heat,
        vibration=cylinder.vibration,
        reconnection_rate=measure_reconnection_rate(cylinder, prism),
    )

    # 4. Choose mode
    if instability_risk > 0.85:
        mode = "emergency_dump"
        gate.open_dump_path()
        prism.reduce_field_strength()
        cylinder.apply_magnetic_braking()

    elif demand == "pulse" and instability_risk < 0.45:
        mode = "pulse_shaper"
        prism.lock_phase_to_geometry()
        gate.route_to_output_bus()

    elif demand == "brake":
        mode = "passive_brake"
        prism.apply_counter_rotation(low_strength=True)
        gate.route_to_recovery_bus()

    elif phase_error > 0.35:
        mode = "lazarus_rephase"
        prism.smooth_phase_transition()
        gate.limit_output()

    else:
        mode = "hold_ready"
        prism.maintain_counter_field()
        gate.standby()

    return {
        "mode": mode,
        "inversion_strength": inversion_strength,
        "instability_risk": instability_risk,
    }
9. Failure Modes
9.1 Shear Bloom

The interface becomes too turbulent.

Result:

- plasma instability
- heat spike
- loss of confinement
- uncontrolled discharge
9.2 Reconnection Spike

Magnetic fields reconnect too quickly.

Result:

- sudden energy release
- coil overload
- arc formation
- pulse distortion
9.3 Prism Phase Slip

The prism loses phase lock.

Result:

- erratic discharge
- oscillation
- vibration
- energy reflected back into cylinder
9.4 Backwash

The discharge gate reflects energy back into the storage cylinder.

Result:

- pressure surge
- field resonance
- possible containment breach
9.5 Thermal Fracture

The prism or end cap heats unevenly.

Result:

- cracking
- material failure
- seal loss
- plasma-wall contact
10. Safety Constraints
1. Never maximize spin difference and polarity gradient at the same time.
2. Never pulse while phase error is high.
3. Always maintain a passive dump path.
4. Always monitor wall temperature and vibration.
5. Always keep discharge gate impedance matched.
6. Never allow the shear seam to migrate into the storage cylinder.
7. Never let output reflection exceed safe threshold.
