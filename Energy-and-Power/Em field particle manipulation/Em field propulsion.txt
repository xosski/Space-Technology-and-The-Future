Electrodynamic Tether / Current Loop Coupling to Earth’s B-Field (LEO)
1) Objective

Demonstrate propulsion (or drag modulation) in Low Earth Orbit by running current through a conductor to generate Lorentz force in Earth’s magnetic field.

3) PoC Architectures

Option 1: Electrodynamic Tether (classic, highest force)

long conducting tether (hundreds of meters to km scale)

electron emitter/collector to close circuit with ionosphere

current driven by orbital motion (EMF) or power system

Option 2: Superconducting Loop (compact, but harder)

superconducting loop produces strong local magnetic moment

interacts with external field gradients (weak in LEO)

generally less effective than tether unless loop is large and currents are extreme

Recommended PoC: tether-based, because it has heritage and measurable effects.

4) System Block Diagram (Tether PoC)

Power + current control

Attitude determination and control

Navigation + orbit determination (GPS + precise tracking)

5) Ground / Simulation Phase (before flight)

Magnetic field model integration (IGRF) + orbital propagation

Plasma environment estimates (ionosphere density varies with time/solar cycle)

Current closure feasibility (contact resistance is a big deal)

9) Risks & Challenges

tether dynamics (oscillation, libration, snap)

arcing / charging / contactor failure

variable ionosphere conductivity (night/day, solar activity)

attitude coupling: force depends on tether orientation

10) Expected Performance (order-of-magnitude)

With a long tether and modest current, forces can be meaningful compared to photon thrust:

photon thrust at 1 kW: ~3.3 µN

tether forces can reach mN-scale depending on length/current/orientation (highly system-dependent)
