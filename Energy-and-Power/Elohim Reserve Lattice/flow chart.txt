That makes sense because each piece already has a different role in your docs:

Aegis Skin is strongest as the distributed surface that harvests, buffers, recaptures, and routes energy during failures rather than pretending to be a perpetual source.

Ghost Inducer is strongest as the coherent inner heart that organizes a field so the outer shell can harvest something disciplined instead of raw chaos. It is a field-conditioning core, not just storage.

Elohim-ΔX is already described as both an active generator and a reactive absorption module, with a CPSS core that can soak excess flow and reroute it during surge conditions. That makes it a very natural candidate for a distributed emergency battery / buffer node.

So the cleanest interpretation is:

Functional chain
1. Aegis Skin gathers and conditions

The hull or surface layer collects available energy, recovers part of thermal burden, and pushes conditioned output into the reserve network. During abnormal events, it also helps catch transient waste instead of letting it vanish.

2. Ghost Inducer organizes and drives

A Ghost Inducer-type core can act as the central coherence engine that spins or excites the field state feeding the rest of the system. Its job is not to be “the battery,” but to make incoming or internal energy more legible and harvestable.

3. Elohim cells store and absorb

The Elohim inductance cells then become the distributed reserve organs:

they hold charge
they absorb overload
they take excess energy when the main bus is stressed
they can later rerelease that energy in a controlled way

That is a much stronger architecture than asking one big battery bank to do everything.

Why this is coherent

Because it separates three different jobs:

collection / recapture
coherence / induction
storage / surge absorption

That same separation is what makes Ghost Inducer coherent in the first place: heart, organ, skin.
And it matches Elohim-ΔX, where the unit is not only storing energy but also acting as a reactive absorption module under overload.

Best way to frame the Elohim cells

They are probably best described as:

inductive reserve cells
surge-buffer batteries
distributed emergency capacitive organs
energy soak / rerelease nodes

Not just passive batteries.

That fits especially well with the Elohim text, which already gives them:

dynamic capacitance
field-triggered activation
inverse-oscillation absorption mode
output rerouting during catastrophic discharge
What “spin the Elohim cells” should mean

The safest and clearest way to say this is not literal perpetual self-spinning batteries.

It is better framed as:

the Ghost Inducer / Aegis reserve system excites or charges the inductive state
the Elohim cells enter an active capture condition
the cells then store, buffer, and dissipate excess load as needed

That keeps the logic physically cleaner.

Strongest system benefits

This gives you:

distributed reserve instead of one single battery room
better surge capture
staged rerelease into damaged buses
local sector survivability
less wasted transient energy
better overload diffusion across the ship
Best one-line doctrine

Use Aegis Skin and Ghost Inducer as the harvest-and-coherence layer that excites the reserve network, then let Elohim-ΔX inductance cells act as distributed batteries and surge-buffer organs that store, absorb, diffuse, and later reroute excess energy across the ship.
