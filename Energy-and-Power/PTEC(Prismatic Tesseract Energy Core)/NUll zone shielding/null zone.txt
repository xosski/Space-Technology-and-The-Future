What counter-rotation would actually do for shielding

Think of the PTEC as a 3D field transformer. When an external wave hits, the danger is coupling — the enemy wave finding a resonant path into your inner chamber/hab volume.

Counter-rotating the outer rails (or rotating inner/outer layers opposite directions) can help in 3 practical ways:

1) Phase scrambling to prevent resonance capture

If the attacker tries to “ring” your geometry (hit the natural frequency), counter-rotation makes the resonant target non-stationary.

The incoming wave can’t stay matched to a moving phase boundary.

Result: less energy couples inward, more gets dumped into controlled loss paths (heat, vented plasma sheath, sacrificial capacitors, etc.).

2) Active cancellation (“anti-wave”) at the boundary

If you can sense the incoming pulse quickly enough, you can do what noise-cancelling headphones do:

detect phase

generate an opposing phase response

Counter-rotation gives you a fast way to shift your emitted boundary phase without needing insane instantaneous power spikes. You’re basically using mechanical/field motion to “dial” phase.

3) Stress distribution + collapse delay

Even if you can’t fully cancel, counter-rotation spreads the load so no single containment segment takes the entire hit long enough to fail.

That matches your safety language: resonance thresholds, overload locks, and dampeners. Rotation becomes a temporal load balancer.
