Proof-of-Concept
Lazarus Pulsewheel Core
Magnetically phased flywheel-heart for shipwide pulse-power stabilization
Core Idea

The Lazarus Pulsewheel Core is a large rotating maglev flywheel system surrounded by individually controlled electromagnetic nodes. These nodes pulse in timed sequence around the ring, like a mechanical-electromagnetic heartbeat.

It does not create infinite power.

It stores, preserves, smooths, and releases energy using:

rotational inertia
magnetic suspension
phased electromagnetic pulsing
regenerative braking
distributed reserve routing
SCIL lattice integration
One-Line Doctrine

The reactor gives the first heartbeat; the Pulsewheel preserves the rhythm.

Operating Modes
Mode 1 — Pulse Sustain

Maintains steady rotor speed using timed EM pulses.

Mode 2 — Burst Discharge

Dumps stored flywheel energy into a high-demand system.

Best for:

shield recharge
emergency thrust
weapon ignition
reactor restart
black-start recovery
Mode 3 — Regenerative Brake

Slows the rotor while converting motion back into usable power.

Mode 4 — Surge Sink

Absorbs excess power from shields, reactors, or overload events.

Mode 5 — Lazarus Recovery

Emergency stabilization mode.

The ring applies counter-phased magnetic braking to stop runaway spin, wobble, or resonance.

Why It Works

This is basically a futuristic flywheel battery with maglev control.

It can plausibly:

reduce reactor strain
smooth peak loads
store burst energy
recover braking energy
stabilize shipwide power flow
buy time during reactor brownout

It cannot:

spin forever
create free energy
ignore heat losses
replace the reactor permanently
Primary Failure Modes
Rotor Overspeed

The flywheel exceeds material limits and tears itself apart.

Magnetic Phase Slip

Pulse timing desynchronizes, causing vibration or power loss.

Eddy Current Heating

Magnetic fields induce unwanted heat in surrounding structures.

Bearing Field Collapse

Maglev suspension fails, causing catastrophic rotor contact.

Resonance Bloom

The rotor and field lattice amplify each other destructively.

Development Path
Phase 1 — Small Rotor Bench Test

Build a low-energy magnetic flywheel simulator.

Goal: prove stable magnetic acceleration and braking.

Phase 2 — Phased Pulse Ring

Test sequential electromagnet activation around the rotor.

Goal: produce controlled “heartbeat” rotation.

Phase 3 — Regenerative Output

Use braking coils to recover energy.

Goal: measure conversion efficiency.

Phase 4 — Surge Absorption Demo

Feed a simulated overload into the rotor system.

Goal: convert surge into stored rotational energy.

Phase 5 — SCIL Integration Simulation

Model the Pulsewheel as a node inside the larger shipwide lattice.

Goal: test duty-cycle reduction and emergency power behavior.

Final Doctrine

The Lazarus Pulsewheel Core is not a perpetual engine.

It is a magnetic heart.

It takes reactor pulses, stores them as motion, smooths them through controlled electromagnetic rhythm, and gives the ship a way to survive between heartbeats.
