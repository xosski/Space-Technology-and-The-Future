Relationship to Your Existing Concepts

This architecture fits well into your broader conceptual framework:

PTECs act as plasma generators.
HPCC/LPCC becomes the plasma conditioning and shaping stage.
LHPC serves as a storage and regulation capsule for conditioned plasma.
MFHAP uses the conditioned plasma for matter processing and reconstruction concepts.
DPICN distributes the resulting energy or plasma through a network of inductive cells.

One engineering note: the phrase "centrifuge" can be misleading for plasmas because the charged particles are governed primarily by electromagnetic forces rather than by mechanical spinning. A more physically grounded description would be a rotating magnetic confinement chamber or helical magnetic vortex chamber, where rotating magnetic fields induce the plasma's rotational motion.

I think that's the strongest evolution of your idea: instead of viewing the chamber as a literal centrifuge, frame it as a magnetically driven plasma vortex conditioner that uses rotating fields to organize, stabilize, and shape the plasma before it feeds downstream systems. That ties the concept more closely to established plasma physics while still leaving room for the speculative spacecraft architecture you're exploring.
