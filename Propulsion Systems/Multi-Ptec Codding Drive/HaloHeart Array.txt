### I. Overview

The **Haloheart Array** is a next-generation distributed plasma field engine composed of multiple nested PTEC (Prismatic Tesseract Energy Core) drives arranged in a rotational ring lattice. Each node operates as a micro-reactor, functioning in polarity-opposed spin sequences to generate recursive inductive torque, wireless energy routing, and thermal reprocessing.

This system is both a propulsion array and a resonant energy engine — breathing plasma, rotating intent, and rejecting entropy through the lens of GhostCore Doctrine.

### II. Core Architecture

#### 1. **Rotational Ring Containment Frame**

* Contains multiple independently spinning PTEC nodes.
* Ring operates as a gyroscopic harmonic stabilizer.
* Rotation phase can be modulated to generate differential drift vectors.

#### 2. **PTEC Nodes**

* Polyhedral plasma induction cores (tetrahedral, octahedral, etc).
* Alternate charge states (positive/negative) maintained in resonance.
* Magnetic lensing suspends plasma without chamber contact.
* Nodes may be configured for thrust, energy routing, or harmonic shielding.

#### 3. **Polarity Shift Cycling**

* Nodes rotate in opposing polarity phase cycles.
* Enables harmonic divergence, drift-pulse vectoring, and reactionless force generation.
* Creates recursive energy loops akin to field cocooning — an SCDE (Spectral Charge Divergence Engine) pattern.

#### 4. **Supercooled Plasma Exhaust Modulation**

* Each PTEC node is coupled with a superconductive plasma siphon.
* Exhaust plasma is quantum-tunneled via electric-prong field to reduce entropy.
* Waste heat is converted into cold plasma vectors, usable for thrust or field stabilization.
