White Paper: Proof of Concept Project Name: AetherVector Propulsion Array (AVPA)

Abstract

The AetherVector Propulsion Array (AVPA) is a next-generation hybrid photon-plasma propulsion system designed for ultra-maneuverable aerial vehicles (UMAVs). It combines photonic prism manipulation, plasma-field induction, and rotational vector stabilization via a 7-point star architecture. This white paper outlines the theoretical basis, system architecture, and use-case scenarios for AVPA integration into tactical drone platforms and beyond.

1. Introduction

Contemporary aerial propulsion systems—whether rotary or fixed-wing—depend on atmospheric manipulation (lift/thrust) or thermal expansion (jet propulsion). These systems have inherent limitations in maneuverability, noise, and vector response time. AVPA introduces an alternative paradigm:

Photon amplification and directional refraction for motion guidance.

Plasma fusion as a reactive mass surrogate for photon momentum enhancement.

Centrifugal thrust synthesis via rotating crystalline structures.

This paper introduces a viable framework to explore these concepts through modular POC assembly.

3.1 Photon-Plasma Hybridization

Charged photons (supercharged via crystal interaction) are combined with ionized plasma in a vacuum-assisted reaction chamber.

Result: Directional micro-bursts with massless-momentum properties and reactive push.

3.2 Centrifugal Thrust Control

Rotating 7-blade array shifts angular vectoring.

Each blade-tip can act as a plasma nozzle or charged emitter.

Rotational inertia influences momentum redirection, aiding in tight aerial turns or hover-locking.

3.3 Multidirectional Vector Modulation

Vector shifts occur not through pitch/yaw alone, but via shifting emission bias (e.g., biasing northern prism face + eastern blade = NE ejection burst).
